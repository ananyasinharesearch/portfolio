About Me

When I first encountered biology in school, I was fascinated by how invisible molecular processes coordinate to drive cellular life. That fascination grew into a desire to understand how biological systems respond to stress and reorganize regulatory networks to shape cellular outcomes.

My academic training has been interdisciplinary, centered on how biological information flows from molecular regulation to cellular function. During my master’s dissertation at AIIMS Delhi, I investigated RNA-level regulation and host susceptibility to infection, gaining experience in PBMC isolation, RNA extraction, cDNA synthesis, cell culture, infection assays, and transcriptomic pathway analysis.

Today, I am focused on RNA-binding proteins and antiviral discovery, combining experimental and computational methods. I am motivated to pursue research on stress-induced RNA processing and intron regulation, with an emphasis on understanding how dynamic post-transcriptional programs shape cellular adaptation.
